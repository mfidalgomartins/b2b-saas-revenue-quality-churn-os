# Executive Summary

## Situation
The company has delivered strong topline recurring growth over the last 36 months, but leadership needs to know whether that growth is durable.

The central question is whether ARR/MRR expansion is driven by healthy recurring fundamentals (retention, price realization, quality expansion) or by fragile mechanics (discount pressure, low-quality acquisition, and concentrated forward churn risk).

## Findings
1. Growth is strong in level terms.
- MRR increased from approximately $3.75M to $9.52M (+154%).

2. Revenue quality is improving but not fully secure.
- Realized price index improved and average discount declined.
- Discount-dependent revenue remains material (about 15.9% in the latest month).

3. Retention is stable but below healthy expansion benchmark.
- Latest GRR is about 99.18% and NRR is about 99.83%.
- NRR near parity indicates limited net expansion surplus after churn/contraction.

4. Churn burden is uneven.
- SMB churn materially exceeds Enterprise.
- Renewal periods show elevated churn risk vs non-renewal periods.

5. Expansion quality is mixed.
- Expansion is net-positive overall, but a meaningful share of expansion is fragile (discount/health pressure signatures).

6. Downside is concentrated.
- Risk is not diffuse across the full portfolio; concentrated account clusters drive disproportionate downside exposure.

## Risks
1. Revenue quality risk behind topline growth.
- Strong MRR growth can conceal fragility if discount dependency remains elevated.

2. Renewal window risk.
- Churn intensity rises around renewal, increasing near-term ARR volatility risk.

3. Concentration-adjusted downside.
- A relatively small set of accounts can drive outsized downside in stress scenarios.

4. QA status is fully green.
- Latest formal validation reports `15 PASS`, `0 WARN`, and `0 FAIL`.
- No high/critical findings are open.

## Recommendations
1. Strengthen discount discipline in high-pressure channels and renewal workflows.
2. Prioritize renewal intervention for high/critical governance-priority accounts.
3. Create SMB-focused stabilization motions (usage adoption, support burden reduction, payment hygiene).
4. Operationalize concentration risk tracking as a standing leadership KPI.
5. Use scenario outputs in monthly planning to quantify downside and intervention upside.

## Next Steps
1. Institutionalize monthly refresh using the release process (`manifest + checksums + validation gate`).
2. Publish dashboard/report pack as the recurring commercial operating review artifact.
3. Add strict pre-event temporal splits for predictive-grade calibration as phase 2.
